Guardian Baltimore

An ongoing, evolving partnership with the local youth-focused dance company.

Context: Ongoing Partnership
Skills: web redesign, social media, data visualization
Timeframe: 05/2016 - Present
The goal:

We've worked with Guardian this past year on many varying projects. Through intial stakeholder interviews with the Director and the rest of the company, we've identified three key business goals to guide us through this partnership:

  • increase audience reach & engagement
  • find new potential members
  • get new sponsorship and performance opportunities
Work

Web Redesign

We worked with Guardian to give their website a more modern organization and feel. My role included reviewing their current site and three competitors for best practices, interviewing stakeholders to determine organizational culture and business goals, crafting an information architecture to allow the site to grow as the company grows, and developing wireframes, prototypes, and usability testing for their new website.

Social Media

We updated the existing Youtube channel with content from recent shows, created new public Facebook and Instagram pages to share engaging content with our audience, and created an internal chat structure to ensure important details aren't missed, while still allowing members to have fun conversations.

For our upcoming Artscape installation, we are working to implement a word-of-mouth outreach strategy, by using a Snapchat geofilter at our location for the duration of the event.

We are looking to add Spotify and Medium to the social media strategy in the future, to provide our audience with music that we dance to and blog posts about issues relevant to us.

Data Visualization

We're creating a datasheet to highlight our successes in youth development. We designed a survey for our students to take at the beginning and end of each semester. Once the results are compiled, we will work together to design the initial data data sheet and a template for future datasheets.

The impact:

Practice

We've gained 5 new members, started a youth lindy hop group, and entered competitions all throughout the region. (Our students have won trophies at a couple of different events.)

Perform

Guardian has been invited to perform at events such as the Freedom Sounds festival, Norma Miller's Birthday Party, and many more.

Our students have begun performing for audiences both on their own and alongside the rest of the company, and are showcasing an increased level of professionalism as a result of it.

Pass On

We've expanded our offerings into two new programs, including Lindy Hop classes at The SEED School of Maryland and Breaking classes at the 29th Street Community Center.

This year, Guardian was selected to host a weekend long dance camp at Artscape.
